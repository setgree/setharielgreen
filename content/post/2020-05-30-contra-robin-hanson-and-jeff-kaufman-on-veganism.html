Observations, and arguments worth responding to:

Michael Huemer observes – in Dialogues on Ethical Vegetarianism, and in a recent Cato Unbound series on What moral considerations do humans owe animals – three things that I agree with and think about a lot:

  • Eating meat is the worst thing that he has ever done (I think this is true for me as well);
  • Factory farming is at least a plausible contender for the most important ethical issue today;
  • “Given that nearly every person in America buys animal products from factory farms on a regular basis, it should be easy to find someone to defend the morality of that practice. In fact, it is almost impossible to find anyone to do that.”

I personally can think of three people whose writing I like, and whose ideas I take seriously, who have given reasons why they (or conditions under which they will) eat animal products:1 Scott Alexander, Robin Hanson, and Jeff Kaufman.2 I’m going to skip over Scott’s piece for now and respond to Robin and Jeff.

I disagree with all three men, but for what it’s worth, I’m glad they wrote about their thinking. It’d be a much worse state of affairs, much harder to push the needle, if people who eat meat refused to talk about it. Putting your reasons out there sets terms for conversation and possible persuasion. I’m reminded of a tumblr post (that I can’t find right now) about parents of surly teenagers commenting: “look who finally decided to join us.” If the thing you want is for the kid to join you, don’t isolate or belittle them when they do!3 Same principle applies here, I think. I believe that these guys are wrong, and what they’re doing is bad, and when I read their arguments, I feel sad, thinking about all the miserable animals. But I want to live in a world where more people talk about this stuff, and I’m trying to do my part to make that happen. If I scream at the men when they voice their opinions, I don’t think I’m helping. So, to them, and everyone who writes about the subject, thanks for sharing. Here’s what I think.
