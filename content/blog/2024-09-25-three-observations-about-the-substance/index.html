--- title: Four observations about "The Substance" author: Seth Green date: '2024-09-25' slug: four-observations-about-the-substance categories: [] tags: [] ---

  1. I would have enjoyed this movie a bit more with some tighter editing, less repetition, and more leavening humor. But I did enjoy it.

  2. This movie is basically Fight Club with no good/bad distinction between the halves of the self. (Although Fight Club is also a little confused about this because it’s so horny for Brad Pitt.)

  3. Body horror, as a genre, has a lot of built-in audience punishment. The strange thing about this movie was its cvontrasting quantity of titillating material. Is one meant to compensate for the other? Perhaps the movie is saying: “Here are 200 shots of Margaret Qualley’s ass. We dare you to stare. Now here’s a monster spraying blood on a studio audience. That’s what you get!” I found this a little hard to process.

  4. I liked the the Elizabeth-making-food scene because it makes the connection between borrowing-the-flesh-of-another-to-stay-young and eating meat explicit. Blood sausage is literally vampiric.
