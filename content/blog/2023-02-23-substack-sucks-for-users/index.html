--- title: "Substack sucks" author: "Seth Green" date: "2023-02-23" slug: "substack-sucks" categories: [] tags: [] ---

I notice that a lot of writers I like have moved their blogs to substack.

I presume they’re getting a lot of money for this – Danny Lavery was kind enough to tell the NYT that his 2-year contract was worth $430,000. Danny currently has “over 16,000 subscribers” so I can see why he found this deal attractive.1

But as a reader, Substack sucks. When I click a blogpost, it’s because I want to read it, not

  • share my email

  • pay a bunch of money for a product I don’t understand

  • post about it on my “profile”

  • recommend it to strangers

  • or share it on Twitter.

All of these things help Substack make money and add friction to my reading experience. We get used to these things gradually – that’s how the shitty technology adoption curve works. But the UX sucks.

I hope we return to more open technology and a more open blogging culture.

This essay was inspired by my inability to comment on Robin Hanson’s blog without becoming a paid subscriber.

  1. “Ludicrously overpriced in a way that encapsulates the era of easy VC money” is the phrase that came to mind, and I’m a paid subscriber!↩︎
